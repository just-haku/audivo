# TurboVideo - Automated Video Creator

TurboVideo is an automated video generation platform that converts text topics or structured scripts into ready-to-publish short-form (vertical 9:16) or long-form (horizontal 16:9) videos. It couples modern local AI voice generation (TTS), speech-to-text alignment (ASR), large language models (LLMs), and FFmpeg-driven video processing into a cohesive, user-friendly tool.

---

## 🚀 Quick Start

1. **Configure Environment:**
   Create a `.env` file in the root directory to define Docker resources:
   ```env
   DOCKER_MEM=12G
   DOCKER_CPUS=10.0
   APP_PORT=8000
   ```
2. **Start the Application:**
   Run the startup script:
   ```bash
   ./start.sh
   # Or in Docker directly:
   docker compose up -d
   ```
3. **Access the Web Interface:**
   Open [http://localhost:8000](http://localhost:8000) in your web browser.

## 📖 Complete User Guide

TurboVideo provides an intuitive, step-by-step workflow designed to take you from a raw idea to a completed video in minutes. Below is a detailed walkthrough of all options and features available in the interface.

### Step 1: Input Video Script & AI Scriptwriter
At the top of the configuration panel, you define the spoken content for your video:
* **AI Script Writer:**
  * **Provider:** Select from Gemini, Groq, DeepSeek, xAI, or a local Ollama instance.
  * **Model Name:** Input the model name (e.g., `gemini-1.5-flash`, `deepseek-chat`, etc.).
  * **Topic/Keyword:** Type in your video's core concept and click **Write Script** to have the LLM automatically draft a formatted script.
* **Script Textbox:** Directly paste your pre-written script or the output generated by the AI writer.
* **VieNeu-TTS VIP Panel:**
  * When using local VieNeu-TTS v3, a specialized panel becomes active.
  * Click to insert expressive cues directly into the text, such as `[cười]` (laughter), `[thở dài]` (sigh), or `[hắng giọng]` (throat-clearing). The synthesizer will render these natural human expressions inline with the text!
* **Plain Text to XML / Load Sample:** Use these helpers to format your text or load a pre-configured template. Click **Confirm Script** when ready.

---

### Step 2: Media Materials Pool
Define the visual footage and background music tracks for your video:
* **Downloader / Stock Searcher:**
  * Paste a video/audio URL, or search for stock keywords (e.g., "city life", "calming forest").
  * Use the dropdown to choose between **Video Footage**, **Background Music**, or **Subtitle Only**, then click **Fetch / Find**.
* **Upload Local Files:**
  * Drag or upload files directly from your computer:
    * **Video Clips:** Stock clips or footage (MP4, MKV, AVI).
    * **Background Music:** Audio tracks (MP3, WAV, M4A).
    * **Custom Font:** Add customized subtitle typography (.ttf, .otf).
    * **Intro / Outro Videos:** Video templates that will be appended to the start or end of your compiled video.
    * **Logo / Watermark:** Overlay a logo (PNG, JPEG).
* **Media Selection & Categories:**
  * **Categorize Assets:** Enter a category name and apply it to selected video clips to group them.
  * **Video Pool:** Check/uncheck individual clips to include or exclude them from the compilation pool.
  * **Background Music:** Select the background track to mix with the voiceover.

---

### Step 3: Production & Style Settings
Fine-tune the output formatting, voiceover, and styling details:
* **Voice Configuration:**
  * **Voice Language:** Select your target language.
  * **Voice Model:** Choose from local/offline neural voice models (like Ngọc Linh, Minh Quang, etc.) or Cloud TTS. Click **Preview** to listen to the voice.
* **Aspect Ratio:** Choose **Vertical (9:16)** for TikTok, YouTube Shorts, or Instagram Reels; or **Landscape (16:9)** for standard YouTube videos.
* **Video Speed Multiplier:** Speed up or slow down video playback (from `0.5x` to `3.0x`).
* **Video Clip Order:** Select **In selected order** to compile videos sequentially or **Random clips and offsets** for dynamic cuts.
* **BGM Volume:** Slider to adjust background music levels relative to the voiceover (e.g., `15%` or `20%` is recommended).
* **Mute Video Tracks:** Check to ensure the original audio from input video footage does not clash with the voiceover.
* **Review Subtitles Before Rendering:**
  * If checked, the backend will generate the audio and align subtitles first, then pop open an interactive **Subtitle Review Modal**.
  * You can manually edit the text of each cue, adjust start/end timestamps, and preview changes before confirming the final video render.
* **Subtitle Timing Source:**
  * **ASR (Whisper):** Transcribes audio for exact word-level timing alignment.
  * **Estimated Timing:** Distributes timings instantly using character-length weightings. Safe, requires no GPU/API keys, and never runs out of memory.
* **Subtitle Appearance Customization:**
  * Choose font family, adjust font size, and set colors for primary text, outlines, and shadows.
  * Adjust the **Vertical Margin (MarginV)** to position subtitles higher or lower (e.g., higher for vertical videos to avoid TikTok UI overlap).
  * Enable **Bold** or **Italic** styling. The **Live Subtitle Preview** box updates instantly to show your design.
* **Intro, Outro & Watermark Position:**
  * Select your intro/outro clips and watermark image from the dropdowns.
  * Position the watermark in any of the four corners and adjust its opacity using the slider.
* **Save settings:** Click **Save Production Settings** to persist these configurations.

---

### Step 4: Compiling & Real-Time Monitoring
Once everything is configured, click the large **Compile AI Video** button:
* **Processing Logs:** Follow the terminal window to see step-by-step progress (TTS synthesis, ASR alignment, FFmpeg rendering, and final composition).
* **Cancel/Retry:** If needed, you can cancel an active job or retry a failed run.
* **Performance Monitor Panel:**
  * Displays real-time CPU Usage, RAM Usage, and remaining Disk Space.
  * Shows whether **WebGPU Hardware Acceleration** is active.
  * Includes an interactive `htop`-like process explorer showing exact resource usage of background tasks.
* **Final Output Preview:**
  * Once rendering is complete, the video is rendered in the preview box.
  * Click **Download Final Video** to save the compiled MP4.

## 💻 Performance Optimization Guide (For 16GB RAM Laptops)

Large video and speech generation pipelines are heavily CPU and memory intensive. Running them unchecked on a 16GB RAM laptop can lead to system locks ("Swap Death") or overheating. TurboVideo features automated protections to ensure stable execution.

### 1. Recommended Settings (in Web UI)
In the **System Config** panel, apply these settings for maximum stability:
* **Max CPU Cores/Threads used by VieNeu-TTS:** Set to **`1 Core`** or **`2 Cores`**.
  * *Why:* Leaving remaining cores free ensures that uvicorn (web server), your browser, and the OS desktop interface (mouse pointer) remain fast and responsive throughout generation.
* **Video rendering chunk size (sentences):** Set to **`20` or `30`**.
  * *Why:* Assembly is split into smaller chunks. After each chunk, video memory is flushed and garbage collection is triggered, keeping RAM usage low.

### 2. Tuning `config.json` (Advanced Performance)
You can directly edit the `config.json` file in the root folder to fine-tune your hardware bounds. These settings are protected and will not be overwritten by frontend saves:

| Config Key | Default | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `"resource_guard_enabled"` | `true` | Enables the background resource monitor. |
| `"resource_guard_ram_limit_mb"` | `1500` | Minimum available RAM before pausing. If memory falls below this threshold, the pipeline pauses for 6 seconds to let the OS swap and reclaim memory. |
| `"resource_guard_cpu_load_pct"` | `90` | Max CPU load average percentage. If the average CPU load exceeds this limit, the pipeline pauses to let the CPU cool down (prevents thermal throttling freezes). |
| `"vieneu_subprocess_batch_size"` | `12` | Number of sentences processed per VieNeu-TTS subprocess call, keeping model RAM footprint bounded at ~2.3GB. |
| `"render_max_workers"` | `2` | Maximum concurrent FFmpeg processes spawned for video segments. Capping it at 2 prevents CPU thrashing and thermal lockups. |

## 🎬 Massive Video Generation Guide (10 - 20+ Hours)

Generating a 1,000,000+ words script (100+ hours of video) in a single-shot execution is not recommended due to OS limitations, MoviePy timeline complexity, and the risk of power/laptop suspensions.

### The Recommended "Split-and-Concat" Workflow:

1. **Slice your script:** Break down your massive script into chapters of **5,000 to 10,000 words** (approx. 35 to 70 minutes of video each).
2. **Generate Chapters:** Run each chapter as an individual project. Since the backend caches synthesized audio files, generating them is fast and secure.
3. **Lossless Join (Immediate Concat):**
   Once all chapter MP4s are generated, join them instantly without re-encoding using FFmpeg. This takes less than 2 minutes and consumes 0% extra CPU/RAM:
   
   Create a `list.txt` file:
   ```text
   file '/path/to/chapter1.mp4'
   file '/path/to/chapter2.mp4'
   file '/path/to/chapter3.mp4'
   ```
   
   Execute FFmpeg:
   ```bash
   ffmpeg -f concat -safe 0 -i list.txt -c copy final_full_length_video.mp4
   ```

## 📝 Subtitle Timing Heuristics

When local ASR (Whisper) is not installed, TurboVideo calculates subtitle timings using **character-length proportional distribution**:

$$\text{duration}_{\text{subtitle}} = \left(\frac{\text{length}_{\text{subtitle}}}{\text{length}_{\text{total}}}\right) \times \text{duration}_{\text{total}}$$

* **Punctuation & Conjunction Splitting:** Long sentences are automatically split at punctuation boundaries (commas `,`, colons `:`, semicolons `;`, dashes `-`, hyphens `–`, etc.) and conjunctions (*và*, *nhưng*, *hoặc*, *nên*, *vì*, *and*, *but*, *so*, *because*) to fit within `max_chars` boundaries.
* **Precision:** By splitting paragraphs into sentence-level audio files during TTS synthesis, the timing math resets at each sentence boundary. This prevents timing drift, ensuring subtitle synchronization looks natural to the human eye.
